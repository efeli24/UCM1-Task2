Introduction

The state of Texas is made up of seven, very diverse natural regions. Each region offers an abundance of different species of flora and fauna. Many types of terrain are also found. From tall mountains to canyons, from forests to deserts, the state of Texas has it all! Many people travel to Texas for hiking and to enjoy the scenery overall.

Region Name Major City Information
Big Bend Country El Paso, TX Home to Big Bend National Park which covers more than 800,000 acres
Hill Country Austin, TX There are many springs and lakes, including an underground lake in the Edwards Aquifer
South Texas Plains San Antonio, TX The subtropical Rio Grande Valley is an ecosystem not found anywhere else in the United States
Panhandle Plains Lubbock, TX Called Panhandle because it resembles a handle with the rest of the state below looking like a pan
Gulf Coast Houston, TX Stretches for hundreds of miles along the Gulf of Mexico
Pineywoods Beaumont, TX Made up primarily of a thick forest of pines
Prairies & Lakes Dallas, TX Fossils of 17 different types of dinosaurs have been found in the region

Overview

With a total population of over 30 million, these are the top 5 cities in the state of Texas by population:

  1. Houston - 2.326 million
  2. San Antonio - 1.493 million
  3. Dallas - 1.345 million
  4. Austin - 964,254
  5. Fort Worth - 895,008

Other tidbits of information:

  • Admitted to the Union on December 29, 1845
  • Largest metro area is Dallas-Forth Worth
  • Total area is 268,596 sq. miles
  • Highest point in the state is Guadalupe Peak at 1,700 feet
